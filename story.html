What a ride.

From a surprise silver medal at Indian Wells to twelve courts under the Kaʻanapali sun — how the Rolfes family built OHANA Pickleball Club, Maui.

2018Newport Beach begins
2021The HUB Pickleball
2025OHANA Maui · Phase One
2026Full 12-court facility

I'll start by saying: what a ride. Jennifer and I were first introduced to pickleball by her father while he was visiting us from Texas. After leaving for a couple of days to go to Indian Wells, he came home with a silver medal in the 2018 USAP National Championship.

“Silver medal? Nationals? Pickleball? What's pickleball?” And so the journey began…
2018 · Newport Beach

Four cracked courts

With our three children — Reid, Emery and Clay — away in college, I started to sell off pieces of my 35+ year-old manufacturing company, Cafejo, which I'd started in 1989. Thinking we were headed down a “semi-retirement” path, Jennifer and I joined the local tennis club across the street (Members #24 and #25). It had four less-than-perfect pickleball courts, and we started to play — and travel — for pickleball.

Like most who play the game, we fell in love with it. We began building a community around those four Newport Beach courts and soon found ourselves running leagues, organizing play and creating social events. I helped the club build more courts to support the community we were creating — and to help the struggling club. Once there were four, there were now twelve. Newport was on its way.

Newport Beach Pickleball Events

One of the largest in the country

At that point I figured I'd done my good deed, and the club's operator now had enough pickleball business to support a Director of Pickleball. Instead, he asked me to stay. Not wanting a job, I came up with a win/win: with the club and the two smart, hard-working women who'd helped build it from the beginning, we started Newport Beach Pickleball Events LLC.

We grew the club to over 1,500 members and ran a profitable events company that fit a huge demand. We kept expanding — building out a total of 31 pickleball courts and a restaurant, which my sons Reid and Clay helped build. We became one of the largest pickleball clubs in the country, running huge tournaments and hosting the PPA, MLP and APP. Those were fun times, for sure.

The business of pickleball

An entrepreneur's playground

It was very early in the business of pickleball, and I saw it as an entrepreneur's playground. Jennifer and I invested in MLP (Major League Pickleball) and DUPR (the Dynamic Universal Pickleball Rating system). I co-founded and was Chairman of the Board of PickleballSuperstore.com. I trademarked and sold the World Series of Pickleball to Andre Agassi for shares in his company. And I helped shape the USAP National Championship by creating the Golden Ticket National Qualifiers — I ran the first one in Newport Beach, ran it for two years, and sold the rights to the APP, who still runs it today.

2021 · The HUB

From abandoned courts to a brand

In 2021, a pickleball friend and I found an abandoned tennis facility in San Diego and developed and operated the first HUB Pickleball. Over the next four years we strategically found property to develop and manage three more HUBs: Silicon Valley, Alameda and Jacksonville. Today, The HUB Pickleball is one of the most respected brands in the industry.

2025 · Maui

We packed our bags

In 2025, I found an opportunity to build a club in Maui. With family ties to Lahaina — and believing pickleball can help a community still recovering from the 2023 fire — Jennifer and I packed our bags and moved to Maui to build a club and give “semi-retirement” another try.

Our sons came with us. After a month, Reid went back to help run The HUB – Silicon Valley, but Clay gave up his manager job at The HUB – San Diego to move to Maui and build — and now manage — the club we built together. In January 2026, Reid moved to Maui too, to help build and run the club.

OHANA Maui was literally created by family.
Created by family

Join our ohana

We now consider some of the Hawaiians and Tongans who helped build it family as well. We officially opened Phase One on September 1, 2025, and the rest of this beautiful twelve-court facility opened in January 2026.

Like I said — what a ride. And it's not over. We look forward to having you and your ohana join our pickleball ohana here in Kaʻanapali, Maui.

Mahalo,
Rolfes OhanaPat, Jen, Reid, Emery & Clay
